First Latin Declension Exceptions

1. Masculine Nouns (Occupations)

While most first-declension nouns are feminine, those denoting occupations are masculine:

  • agricola, -ae (m.): farmer
  • nauta, -ae (m.): sailor
  • poēta, -ae (m.): poet
  • pirāta, -ae (m.): pirate

2. Dative/Ablative Plural (-ābus)

Nouns denoting females use -ābus to distinguish from their masculine counterparts:

dea (goddess) → deābus (to avoid confusion with deīs)
fīlia (daughter) → fīliābus (to avoid confusion with fīliīs)

3. Genitive Singular (-ās)

An archaic form preserved in certain legal and domestic terms:

  • pater familiās: head of the household
  • māter familiās: mother of the family

4. Genitive Plural (-um)

Occasionally found in poetry and Greek-derived measurements:

  • amphora → amphorum
  • drachma → drachmum
